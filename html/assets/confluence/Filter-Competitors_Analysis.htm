    Filter - Competitors, Big Player, Suppliers Analysis

    Skip to end of metadata

    Summary

    All

    Competitors

    • Competitors have more filter options than suppliers
    • Most of them have the filter on the left side
    • The filter groups are quite similar
    • Some competitors have extra filter for insurance or price
    • Some filter use an outstanding design to get into the forcus of the users.

    Suppliers

    • Suppliers in general have less filters than our competitors
    • This filters have their position mostly on top of the search results.
    • Many suppliers are using a grid view to show their cars, but do not have an option to change to list view

    Big Player eCommerce

    Recommendations

    Filter Positions

    Our Rentalcars search filter got a lot more information to handle. It is a lot more, than suppliers have to work with. All websites that have to handle a comparable amount of information like Rentalcars.com have a similar filter pattern. Sort and some extra important stuff on top of the search results. Filter for more refinement are always on the left.

    We should stay in general with the filter pattern we have.

    Interaction elements

    The most used interaction elements are the checkbox and the "Sort by" dropdown. To stand out a very focusing pattern is the price slider.

    Our general approach of our interaction elements is the right choice

    Most of the successful eCommerce Websites have an option to switch between grid and list view. Most of them use grid view as their primary search result view. Our big car hire suppliers do most use a grid view to show their cars.

    We should start offering our customers a grid view of our search results

    Design

    Successful competitors, suppliers and great eCommerce companies have a very reduced design language. It is strong on colour contrast and easy to understand typo hierarchy.

    We should reduce our design Elements to the necessary

    We should install headlines where necessary to improve a better understanding for customers how to interact

    The interaction elements offer a high colour contrast on interaction events.

    We should improve our colour contrast for interaction activity
